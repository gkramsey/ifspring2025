Southern Baked Macaroni and Cheese

Creamy baked southern style macaroni and cheese :)
Sourced from The Hungry Bluebird

Ingredients

  • 8-10 ounces elbow macaroni
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 12 ounces evaporated milk
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons white pepper
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 stick butter
  • 2 tablespoons sour cream
  • 1/2 pound Velveeta
  • 8 ounces shredded Colby-Jack cheese
  • 4 ounces shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mild cheddar cheese

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Bring large pot of water to boil and then salt generously. Cook macaroni just shy of package directions, al dente. Drain well and palce in 9" by 13" baking dish or pan.
  2. In same pasta pot, whisk together whole milk, evaporated milk, eggs, salt, pepper and sugar until combined. Pour mixture over cooked macaroni in pan.
  3. Add butter, sour cream, Velveeta, Colby-Jack and sharp cheddar cheeses. Stir to combine well and top evenly with shredded mild cheddar.
  4. Bake for 35 to 40 minutes until bubbly and lightly browned on top. Let rest for 10 minutes or so before serving. Enjoy!

Other Recipe Sites

  • New York Times Cooking: Contains a homepage to browse recipes, as well as various categories for holidays, food styles, moods etc. Image focused to allow user to see what the end product will look like, and also includes cook time without having to navigate to a specific recipe page.
  • Blue Apron Also image focused, and also has the ability to filter by main ingredient and cuisine type. Has search functionalty for those who know what they are looking for. Also has clear markers for vegetarian dishes. Clean UI.
  • Budget Bytes Focused on the cost of the recipe, which is a nice addition. Shows the cost breakdown of the entire recipe as well as per serving. Has similar filters as the previous websites, but also adds a cost option. Again, very simple and clean UI that places the emphasis on what the food will look like, drawing the user in.

Other Non-Recipe Sites

  • GQ Sports Simple and clean UI, great use of white space without feeling to empty. An unobtrusive nav bar that makes it easy to get from page to page.
  • Supreme Incredibly image focused, going so far as to only show images on the main shop page without any text. Organized in a unique layout, and could be used to draw some inspiration from.
  • Spotify Has a unique homepage and recommendation features, which could be interesting to implement for a recipe website. The sidebar design is also nice, and could be incorperated to house category selection or other similar elements.
